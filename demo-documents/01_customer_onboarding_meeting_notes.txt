Northstar Retail customer onboarding working session
Date: 2026-07-10
Attendees: Maya (Sales), Jordan (Revenue Operations), Priya (Implementation), Leo (Billing), and Sam (Customer Support)

Purpose
Understand why new enterprise customers are taking too long to reach their first successful order.

Current process
1. The Sales representative marks an opportunity as Closed Won in Salesforce after the contract is signed.
2. Revenue Operations receives a Salesforce notification and manually creates the company, contacts, and contract summary in HubSpot.
3. Revenue Operations copies the account name, legal entity, billing address, subscription tier, and promised launch date into a shared onboarding spreadsheet.
4. The Implementation Manager is assigned manually in a weekly spreadsheet review. If the deal closes on Friday, assignment frequently waits until Monday.
5. The Implementation Manager emails the customer for a technical contact, a purchase order, and preferred kickoff times.
6. Billing creates a customer account in NetSuite only after a valid purchase order arrives. Billing often learns that a purchase order is missing after the kickoff has already been scheduled.
7. The Implementation Manager creates a project plan in Asana, schedules the kickoff, and asks Customer Support to prepare the help-centre access list.
8. Sales receives a Slack message when the kickoff is complete. There is no automatic update in Salesforce.

Observed pain points
- Sales data is retyped in HubSpot, the spreadsheet, Asana, and NetSuite.
- The shared spreadsheet is the only view of onboarding status. Different teams overwrite dates and use different status labels.
- Missing purchase orders are discovered late, delaying billing and launch.
- Team members use Slack direct messages to chase ownership because the Salesforce notification does not show a named implementation owner.
- A customer can receive a kickoff invite before their billing account is ready.
- Support access is prepared from a manually maintained email list, so the wrong users can be invited.

Targets and measures
- Assign an Implementation Manager within two business hours of Closed Won.
- Hold the kickoff within two business days when the purchase order and technical contact are complete.
- Reduce manual data re-entry by at least 80 percent.
- Do not schedule a kickoff until billing readiness is confirmed.

Risks noted by the team
- Incorrect legal entity or billing address can cause invoices to be rejected.
- A promised launch date can be missed when an opportunity is not included in the weekly review.
- Customer contact details can be stale when copied from a personal Sales spreadsheet.
- There is no auditable record of who changed an onboarding date or why.
