Welcome to FlowLens: an AI-powered business process analysis and transformation copilot.

I built this with Codex, using GPT Five Point Six as a hands-on full-stack engineering partner. Together, we established a production-quality foundation: React and TypeScript on the frontend; FastAPI, PostgreSQL, and Docker on the backend; plus health checks, unit tests, and browser-based verification.

A consultant signs in and creates a focused transformation project. This example, Northstar, is working to reduce accounts-payable exceptions and speed up finance operations.

They can add meeting notes, SOPs, spreadsheets, PDFs, or Word documents. FlowLens stores those files in the project, extracts the relevant evidence, and makes it ready for analysis.

The AI layer is deliberately designed as independent services, rather than one giant prompt. I moved the provider to Claude through the Anthropic Messages API and structured outputs. Separate services create the process model, executive summary, opportunity analysis, and implementation roadmap.

The report is a decision-ready view of the current state: the process map, people and systems involved, pain points, risks, and prioritised AI and automation opportunities. Each opportunity includes implementation detail, user stories, and acceptance criteria.

The roadmap then converts the recommendations into practical delivery phases. Project chat can stay grounded in the uploaded evidence and the completed report.

Codex accelerated the full build loop. I used GPT Five Point Six to shape the architecture, implement and test the product, prepare realistic source documents, and verify the finished experience with Playwright. The result is FlowLens: a focused SaaS workflow for turning process evidence into transformation decisions.
